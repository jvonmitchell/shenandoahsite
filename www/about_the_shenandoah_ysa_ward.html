About the Potomac Single Adult Ward

The Mt. Vernon Virginia Stake presidency saw a need for the formation of a midsingles ward in large part because of the growing number of midsingles in the Washington DC Metropolitan area. The need was presented to the First Presidency of the Church, which later recieved divine revelation that a midsingles ward should be built on the East Coast. The Potomac Single Adult Ward was born and would be the first of its kind on this side of the United States. The first sacrament meeting was held January 10, 2010.

The boundaries for this ward includes both the Mt. Vernon Virginia Stake and the Annandale Virginia Stake. Special exceptions must be approved by the stake presidents.


View Potomac Ward Boundaries in a larger map

Plans are currently underway to unite all of the single wards of the Mt. Vernon Virginia Stake and place them under one roof. A building has been designated for this purpose. It is currently under renovations and will be open for business in 2011.

All 3 singles wards: Colonial I, Colonial II, and the Potomac Ward will have sacrament meetings in this building, which has 3 floors and no primary or nursery rooms. This building is designed specifically for the singles.

Since this ward began in 2010, it has doubled in size. It is currently at 175 members and continues to grow. About 83.4% of its members are endowed adults with a temple recommend.

If you live within the Potomac Ward boundaries, we encourage you to check us out. We would love to have you and hope you will decide to join our ward.
